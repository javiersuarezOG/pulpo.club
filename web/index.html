How we rank

Every listing gets a 0–100 composite score from three plain-English dimensions. Higher is a better-looking opportunity for an investor. The score is meant as a starting point — not a substitute for talking to a broker and walking the lot.

Price vs Comparable Lots

How cheap this listing is per square meter compared to similar lots in the same area — same zone first, then the wider region if there aren't enough nearby comparisons. Houses are compared against houses and raw land against raw land, so a 6-bedroom mansion doesn't make a vacant lot look expensive. A score of 100 means it's the cheapest comparable; 0 means it's the most expensive.

Location & Accessibility

How well-positioned the spot is. We score the area on zone tier — Tier A is established beach corridors with deep buyer demand, B is proven secondary, C is frontier — and add points for beachfront, paved access, water and power on the lot, and proximity to the nearest international airport. We track both SAL (Comalapa, operational) and AeP (Aeropuerto del Pacífico, planned on the eastern coast) and pick whichever is closer. The eastern coast — Conchagua, La Unión, El Cuco — gets the "near airport" bonus from AeP today, anticipating its opening.

Area Momentum

Whether an area is gaining or losing steam right now. We track how often listings in the area get repriced down — a sign of motivated sellers — and (as we accumulate scraping history) how quickly new inventory appears in each zone. A tier-B area gaining momentum can outscore a tier-A area that's plateaued. Sparse zones with fewer than 5 listings score the neutral default until we have more data.

The composite

composite = 0.40 × Price vs Comps + 0.35 × Location + 0.25 × Momentum

The composite is the single number you see at the top of the listing panel. Each dimension is shown as a bar so you can see how a high or low score was earned, with a one-line explanation under each bar.

Honest limitations

Coverage of physical-attribute flags (water, power, paved access) is still building — bonuses fire only when set, and many listings don't yet carry them. The Momentum signal is single-input today (repriced-rate); listing-velocity will be added once we have 4+ weeks of nightly scrape history. The methodology is intentionally simple: three dimensions an investor can defend out loud, not a black-box opinion.

What each control does

Filters narrow what's shown. The bottom three sliders change the order, not the set.

Zone

Filter to a region (a group of zones) or to a single zone. "No zone" surfaces listings whose location couldn't be parsed.

Price (USD)

Hide listings outside the price range. Listings without a parsed price drop once the range is narrowed from the default.

Size (m²)

Hide listings outside the size range. Sizes are square meters; 1 vrs² ≈ 0.7 m².

Score floor

Hide listings below this score. The score is the same number shown in the table's Score column.

Adjust the ranking — Price vs Comps · Location · Momentum

Three sliders that rebalance the score. Each controls how much its dimension counts; defaults are 40 / 35 / 25 and re-derive on reset. Move a slider right to make that dimension matter more, left to matter less. Pick "Sort by your custom weights" in the sort dropdown to see your re-ranked list — other sort options ignore the weights.
