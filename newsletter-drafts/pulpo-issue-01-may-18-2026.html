Top pick · 01 Working land Coffee · river · trees

17.5-acre coffee farm with a river running through it.

Chiltiupán · La Libertad · 25 min to El Zonte beach
$185,000 · negotiable · paperwork is clean

The listing photos are weak and the title undersells it. What's actually on the property: 11 acres of rust-resistant coffee already producing, 5,000 new seedlings planted this season, the El Zonte river crossing the land with two swimmable natural pools, year-round spring water, electricity, and roughly $50k in standing timber (cedar, mahogany, balsam) by the broker's count. The small casita needs to be gutted.

Three things have to be true
(1) You're fine with a 4×4 in the wet season. (2) You either want to run coffee yourself or you'll pay a manager $400–600/mo to do it. (3) You're not in a hurry to sleep on-site — the house is a 6–12 month project. If all three check, $1.50/m² for productive land this close to El Zonte is a price that doesn't repeat.

Top pick · 02 Move-in ready Rental-friendly

Two-bed at Zonset, two-minute walk to the beach.

El Zonte · Chiltiupán · 105 m² · 2 bed · 2 bath
$445,694 · developer price · no negotiation reported

For a buyer who wants to fly in and unlock a door, this is the strongest finished build in the issue. El Zonte absorbed the 2021–2024 surge and stayed functional — schools, a real grocery, three good restaurants. The Zonset unit is honest construction: exposed concrete, warm wood, a balcony facing greenery instead of the next building.

The rental math
Comparable two-beds in El Zonte clear roughly $180–230/night in high season (Nov–Apr) and $90–110 in low. At 55% occupancy that's gross ~$32k/yr, net ~$22k after HOA and management. About 5% yield, with appreciation as the upside.
The catch
This is a 47-unit development across three towers — real rental supply is coming online at the same time you are. The math holds if the unit will also be personally used 6–10 weeks a year. Pure-investment buyers should run sensitivity on occupancy down to 40%.

Market context

Four things worth knowing this fortnight.

The Litoral is repaved from Km 42 to Km 57. El Tunco to El Zonte is now 22 minutes instead of 40 in traffic. Land valuations in the corridor haven't fully repriced yet — expect that over the next 6–9 months.

Rains started May 12, earlier than 2025. For viewings in Chiltiupán or anywhere off the Litoral, a sedan rental is a $400 mistake. Go with the broker's 4×4 or rent one.

Sunset Park opened on the La Libertad malecón. Real demand signal for the El Obispo / Punta Roca pocket — more family tourism, fewer late-night pickup trucks. Helps values there; little spillover into the Tunco–Zonte corridor.

A viewing trip for this list is realistically 3–4 days. Stay in El Zonte, group brokers by afternoon, allow 90 minutes between properties for the road and the conversation.

One number worth knowing

$45 per vara² — the new floor in the corridor.

Two listings in this issue are priced at exactly $45/vara² (~$65/m²): the corridor plot at Tunco/Sunzal and the Costa Brava cliff in El Zonte. Eighteen months ago the same product was trading at $28–32. Every new listing between Km 52 and Km 60 has cleared $40 in 2026.

Practical implication for the lot picks above: $55–60/vara² by late 2027 is a reasonable base-case exit. Don't underwrite higher without a specific catalyst, and budget for a Q4 dip if the Surf City II infrastructure tranche slows permitting — current broker chatter suggests it will.
